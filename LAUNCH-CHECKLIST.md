## WHAT TO AVOID

🚩 **Week 1-11:**
- Not responding to comments within 60 min
- Asking for upvotes/signal boosting
- Spreading yourself across 10 channels
- Being vague about what you're building
- Waiting for "perfect" before launching

🚩 **Launch Week:**
- Launching to silence (no warm list = disaster)
- Disappearing after Day 1 (founders are invisible)
- Ignoring feedback ("Actually, people are wrong")
- Focusing on PH ranking instead of signups
- Over-promising ("This will be the Figma killer")

🚩 **Week 2+:**
- Shipping random features (build what users ask for)
- Ignoring churn (if >50% leave, you have a problem)
- Treating vanity metrics as success (1000 signups, 10 paying = failure)

## REALITY CHECK

**This is hard.**

- Week 3-4 will feel slow. It's not. You're planting seeds.
- Launch Day will feel chaotic. It should. Everyone's watching.
- Week 2 will have users you don't understand asking why things don't work.
- Week 4 you'll know if this has product-market fit or not.

**Most founders quit Week 3.**

Don't.

The ones who make it: execute this playbook week 1-4, then assess. If Week 4 metrics say "there's real demand," sprint. If they say "this isn't it," pivot. Either way, you'll know.

---

## Emergency Mode (If Launch Doesn't Hit Targets)

**Scenario:** Launch day gets <20 signups. Now what?

**Do NOT:**
- Panic
- Restart feature development
- Blame the product

**Do:**
- Shift to direct outreach mode immediately
- Find 100 people on Reddit/Twitter asking about your problem
- DM: "I built a fix for [problem]. Test it? Tell me if it's broken."
- Convert 1 in 10 = 10 signups

**New goal:** Get to 25-50 signups via direct outreach by Week 3.

Then assess: Is the problem real? Am I talking to the right people? Is my positioning clear?
